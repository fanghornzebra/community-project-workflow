Step 5 — Synthesized feedback document

After all peer reviews are submitted, an AI-generated feedback document (2-3 pages) is produced for each student. This document synthesizes all feedback and includes:

  • Key strengths identified across all reviewers
  • Development areas and suggestions for improvement
  • Cross-cohort insights — ensuring knowledge is shared symmetrically so everyone benefits from good ideas raised in any review

Credit: Fadi — ensures no student misses out on valuable feedback given to someone else.

Discussion — peer review format

What should peer reviewers see and provide feedback on?

Option A

Feedback per section

Reviewers provide a rating and notes for each section — Core Idea, Team Planning, Team Profiles, Budget Planning, Success Criteria. Each section has its own weight.

Pro: Granular, structured. Students know exactly where to improve.
Con: More time per review. May feel rigid.
Option B

Overall notes + rating

Reviewers provide one overall rating and open-ended notes. Simpler, more conversational — like leaving a peer a thoughtful comment.

Pro: Faster, more natural. Lower barrier.
Con: Less structure. Feedback quality may vary.

Other considerations

"What if the voices of those who came before could whisper guidance to those who walk the path today?"

What if we could surface lessons from 15 years of youth-led projects — the wins, the failures, the stories that never made it into any report — and make them available to every student, in real time?

How do we help them avoid the same pitfalls?
How do we pass on what we've learned — not as a document, but as a conversation?

What tendAI does (steps 3–4)

tendAI is an AI advisor trained on paNhari's 15 years of institutional knowledge — successes, failures, and stories that never made it into formal reports.

  • Reviews the project against patterns from hundreds of past youth-led initiatives
  • Asks guided questions: "Have you considered how this scales after the fellowship?"
  • Offers contextual advice: "Teams in Kigali found that engaging local government early doubled completion rates"
  • Cites its sources — real paNhari experience, not generic AI advice

Always opt-in. Enhances peer review — doesn't replace it.
